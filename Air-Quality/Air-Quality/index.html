About Air Pollution

Air pollution in India is a pressing issue, with high levels of harmful pollutants like PM2.5 and PM10 originating from sources such as vehicles, industrial emissions, crop burning, and construction activities. These pollutants pose significant health risks and harm the environment. Reducing emissions and adopting cleaner technologies is essential to improve air quality across the country.

Air Quality Index of Major Cities in India

Indian AQI Standards

The AQI (Air Quality Index) used in India ranges from 0 to 500, with higher values indicating worse air quality. It categorizes air quality into different levels, from "Good" to "Hazardous." These categories help people understand the health impact of current air pollution levels.

Good (0-50)
Moderate (51-100)
Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (101-200)
Unhealthy (201-300)
Very Unhealthy (301-400)
Hazardous (401-500)

PM2.5 Levels

PM2.5, or particulate matter smaller than 2.5 micrometers, is a critical air pollutant affecting human health. These particles penetrate deep into the lungs, leading to respiratory issues, cardiovascular diseases, and other health concerns.

Good (0-12.0 µg/m³)
Moderate (12.1-35.4 µg/m³)
Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (35.5-55.4 µg/m³)
Unhealthy (55.5-150.4 µg/m³)
Very Unhealthy (150.5-250.4 µg/m³)
Hazardous (250.5+ µg/m³)
